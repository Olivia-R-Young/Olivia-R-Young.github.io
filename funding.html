AAS Eclipse Mini-Grant Funding

The building of DLITE Ohio has been graciously funded by the AAS Jay M. Pasachoff Solar Eclipse Mini-Grants Program. Funding was awarded for telescope components, tools, and travel. Visit AAS's website to learn more about the grant program and the other projects they funded!

Contritbutions by the Naval Research Laboratory

We are very thankful to the Naval Research Laboratory (NRL), specifically Joe Helmboldt, for their contributions to this project in the form of telescope components and coaxial cable, and for supplying the backend electronics for the telescope.

Contritbutions by Hillsdale College

We thank Hillsdale College for their donations of coaxial cable, two of our four antennas, and front-end electronics (FEEs) to our project, along with funding team t-shirts for our undergraduate students. We also completed our radio frequency interference (RFI) survey of the site using a Rohde & Schwarz spectrum analyzer donated to Hillsdale College.

Support for PI Olivia Young by the NSF, RIT, and the NRAO

PI Olivia Young is funded through the NSF GRFP under Grant No. DGE-2139292. She also gratefully acknowledges her home institution, RIT, and the NRAO where she is currently working as a visiting student. The freedom, flexibility, and support provided by these institutions and the NSF GRFP allow for the pursuit of independent projects such as building DLITE Ohio.
